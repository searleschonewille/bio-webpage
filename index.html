About

This website is dedicated to the memory of F/L William (Bill) P. Harper and his comrades in arms -- men and women -- who fought with the Royal Canadian Air Force during the Second World War. Upon his death in 1997, Bill Harper left a collection of historical records that details his life and service as a Spitfire pilot in active combat in the European threatre of operations from autumn 1944 until the end of hostilities in May 1945.

This collection includes an oral and written history, his logbook, photograph albums, combat footage, uniforms, decorations and battlefield memorabilia. Also included in this collection are home movies (courtesy of R.W. 'Hap' Beall) that depict Bill Harper and his comrades during the war. These documents comprise a valuable historical record. Special thanks is given to Robert Bracken, the late aviation historian, whose books about Canadian Spitfire pilots inspired Bill Harper and many other pilots and ground crew to tell their stories about the war for the benefit and education of future generations.
